Jean-Michel Basquiat

An American Artist.

A Quick Gander Through A Gallery of His Life

  • 1960's
    • 1960: Born December 22.
    • 1964: Already reading and writing.
    • 1967: Began attending Saint Ann's School, an arts-oriented exclusive private school. While there he and a friend created a children's book. Basquiat was the writer, his friend the illustrator.
    • 1968: Parent's separated. He and his sisters went to live with their father.
  • 1970's
    • 1970: At age 10, his mother was committed to a psychiatric hospital.
    • 1971: By the age of eleven, Basquiat was fully fluent in French, Spanish and English.
    • 1977: Due to his mother's instability and family unrest, Basquiat ran away from home at 15, but ended up returning to his Father's house with help from the police a week later.
    • 1978: Basquiat was expelled from his high school for pieing the principal. His father kicked him out and then he dropped out of school.
  • 1980's
    • 1981: At 21, Basquiat sold his first painting, Cadillac Moon (1981), to Debbie Harry, lead singer of the punk rock band Blondie, for $200.This year he also became the youngest artist to ever take part in documenta in Kassel.
    • 1982: At 22, he was the youngest to exhibit at the Whitney Biennial in New York; he was the first American one-man show at the Annina Nosei Gallery; he befriended Andy Warhol by painting him Dos Cabezas within 2 hours.
    • 1983: He showed up to his show with his girlfriend, the then-unknown singer Madonna.
    • Mid-Eighties: Collaborated with Warhol on a number of pieces; was earning $1.4 million a year and he was receiving lump sums of $40,000 from art dealers; appeared on the cover of The New York Times Magazine; sinking deeper into drug addiciton.
    • 1986-1988: Traveled the world making more paintings, selling them in shows, and he even walked the catwalk at a fashion show in Paris.
    • August 12th, 1988: Found dead from a heroin overdose.
  • After His Death
    • 1992: The Whitney Museum of American Art held a retrospective of his art work.
    • 2017:  Sotheby's auctioned off Untitled, a 1982 painting by Basquiat depicting a black skull with red and yellow rivulets, sold for $110.5 million, becoming one of the most expensive paintings ever purchased.
